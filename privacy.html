🚀 Floaty

Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2025

Floaty is an open-source macOS application. This policy explains what data Floaty accesses and how it is handled.

Data we access

Floaty requests access to your Google account for two purposes:

  • Google Calendar — to read your calendar events and display the current or next upcoming event in the widget. Floaty can also delete events when you check them off.
  • Google Tasks — to read your task lists and mark tasks as completed when you check them off in the widget.

How your data is used

  • Calendar and task data is fetched directly from Google's APIs to your Mac. It is displayed in the widget and used for no other purpose.
  • No data is ever sent to any third-party server. Floaty has no backend.
  • No analytics, tracking, or advertising of any kind.

Data storage

  • OAuth tokens (your Google authorization) are stored in your macOS Keychain — encrypted, local to your device.
  • Widget position and preferences are stored in macOS UserDefaults — local to your device.
  • No data is stored in the cloud or on external servers.

Revoking access

You can revoke Floaty's access to your Google account at any time by visiting myaccount.google.com/permissions and removing "Floaty" from the list of connected apps.

To remove locally stored tokens, open Keychain Access, search for "com.taskfloat", and delete the entries.

Open source

Floaty is fully open source. You can inspect exactly what the app does with your data at any time: github.com/stajulian5/floaty
